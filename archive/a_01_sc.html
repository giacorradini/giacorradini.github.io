The Real Cost of Inflation under Nominally Rigid Wages

Gianmarco Corradini, 15.05.2024

Python Source Code

The following Python script reproduces the savings simulation, the cumulative cost of inflation, the equivalent cost in working hours, and the decline in the real wage.

Python Model

The model is implemented in Python using NumPy for numerical operations and Matplotlib for the graphical representation of the results.

Model Parameters

The annual interest rate is set at 1%. Three annual inflation scenarios are considered: 2%, 5%, and 10%.

The household initially consumes 90% of its monthly nominal wage and therefore saves the remaining 10%. One monthly wage is assumed to correspond to 160 working hours. The principal simulation horizon is 36 months.

Conversion to Monthly Rates

The annual interest and inflation rates are converted into equivalent monthly compound rates.

The monthly interest rate is calculated as:

\[ r_m=(1+r_a)^{1/12}-1 \]

where \(r_a\) is the annual interest rate.

Similarly, the monthly inflation rate is calculated as:

\[ \pi_m=(1+\pi_a)^{1/12}-1 \]

where \(\pi_a\) is the annual inflation rate.

Household Financial Position

The household's financial position is calculated recursively for each month.

At the beginning of the simulation, the financial position is equal to zero. At the end of each month, the household receives one monthly nominal wage, earns interest on its existing savings, and pays the nominal cost of maintaining its original level of real consumption.

The nominal cost of consumption in month \(t\) is:

\[ C_t=0.9(1+\pi_m)^t \]

The household's financial position therefore evolves according to:

\[ S_t = 1 + S_{t-1}(1+r_m) - 0.9(1+\pi_m)^t \]

A positive value of \(S_t\) represents accumulated savings. A negative value represents the financing shortfall that would arise if the household continued maintaining the same level of real consumption after exhausting its savings.

Cumulative Inflation Cost

The cumulative inflation cost measures the additional nominal expenditure required to maintain the initial level of real consumption.

For a period of \(t\) months, the cumulative inflation cost is:

\[ IC_t = 0.9 \left[ \sum_{j=1}^{t}(1+\pi_m)^j-t \right] \]

Using the geometric-series formula, this can be written as:

\[ IC_t = 0.9 \left[ \frac{(1+\pi_m)\left((1+\pi_m)^t-1\right)} {\pi_m} -t \right] \]

The result is expressed in monthly-wage units.

Equivalent Working Hours

The cumulative inflation cost is converted into equivalent working hours by multiplying it by 160:

\[ IC_t^{h}=160IC_t \]

This indicates how many hours of work are required to compensate for the cumulative additional expenditure caused by inflation.

Real-Wage Depreciation

The purchasing power of a constant nominal wage declines as the price level increases.

The proportional change in the real wage after \(t\) months is:

\[ \Delta w_t = \frac{1}{(1+\pi_m)^t}-1 \]

A negative value indicates the percentage loss in the purchasing power of the nominal wage.

Savings Exhaustion

The model identifies the first month in which the household's financial position becomes negative.

Under the selected assumptions, this occurs approximately in:

  • month 127 under 2% annual inflation;
  • month 51 under 5% annual inflation;
  • month 26 under 10% annual inflation.

Graphical Results

The model generates three graphs.

The first graph shows the household's financial position over time under the three inflation scenarios.

The second graph shows the cumulative cost of inflation in monthly-wage units.

The third graph converts the cumulative inflation cost into equivalent working hours.

Numerical Results

After 12 months, the cumulative inflation cost corresponds approximately to:

  • 19 working hours under 2% annual inflation;
  • 46 working hours under 5% annual inflation;
  • 92 working hours under 10% annual inflation.

After 36 months, the cumulative inflation cost corresponds approximately to:

  • 161 working hours under 2% annual inflation;
  • 410 working hours under 5% annual inflation;
  • 841 working hours under 10% annual inflation.

After 36 months, the household's financial position is approximately:

  • 2.634 monthly-wage units under 2% annual inflation;
  • 1.066 monthly-wage units under 5% annual inflation;
  • −1.653 monthly-wage units under 10% annual inflation.

The corresponding real-wage losses after 36 months are approximately:

  • −5.8% under 2% annual inflation;
  • −13.6% under 5% annual inflation;
  • −24.9% under 10% annual inflation.
