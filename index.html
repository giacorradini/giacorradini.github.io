About Me

Hi, I’m Gianmarco Corradini, and I’m passionate about the intersection of economics, statistics, and programming.

I enjoy working with Python, NumPy, Pandas, and Mathematica to explore models in macroeconomics, dynamic systems, and computational economics. I'm particularly interested in how mathematical and programming tools can help describe and simulate complex systems — especially in the context of growth and policy modeling.

Some of the projects published here are based on freelance assignments, which I’ve extended and refined into general tools or explorations. This site is where I collect those results, along with academic and personal work in data analysis and automation.

I enjoy solving problems with logic and structure, and I’m always looking to deepen my understanding through hands-on experimentation — especially where economics and code meet.
